Common Words & Potential Teen Pronunciations (Non-Slang Focus)

    Water: Waw-der (soft 't' to 'd').
    About: Uh-bawt or A-bout (vowel shift).
    Going: Gawn-in' (vowel/ending change).
    Because: Cuz (shortened, common in casual speech).
    Thinking: Thinkin' (dropping 'g').
    Tired: Tired (often drawn out, vowel sound changes).
    You: Yuh or Ya (reduced vowel).
    With: Wif (short 'i').
    Maybe: May-bee (slight vowel emphasis).
    Sorry: Sore-ee or Saa-ree (vowel variation).
    For: Fer (reduced vowel).
    Really: Ree-lee or Rally (vowel change). 

Key Trends (Beyond Specific Words)

    Vowel Shifts: 'i' to 'ee' (milk -> meelk), 'ou' to 'aw' (about -> a-bawt).
    Consonant Changes: 't' becomes 'd' (flap T), 'g' drops at end of '-ing'.
    Vowel Reduction: Unstressed vowels often become schwa or shorter sounds.
    Intonation: Can be more varied, sometimes raising pitch at end of statements (uptalk). 

These aren't strict rules but tendencies in casual, everyday speech, reflecting
linguistic evolution rather than new slang.
